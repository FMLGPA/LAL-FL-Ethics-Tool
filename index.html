What This Tool Is

A legal research assistant providing access to Florida Bar ethics opinions, Florida and federal court decisions, Bar rules, and sample AI use certifications relevant to the ethical use of artificial intelligence in Florida legal practice. Developed by Legal Authority Lab — AI built for law firms, powered by real lawyers.

What This Tool Is Not
This tool does not provide legal advice. Responses are informational only and do not constitute legal counsel, formal ethics opinions, or official guidance from The Florida Bar. Nothing in this tool creates an attorney-client relationship between any user and Legal Authority Lab or any affiliated attorney or firm.

For formal ethics guidance: Florida Bar Ethics Hotline (800) 235-8619
Knowledge Base — Sources & Limitations

Responses are generated solely from a curated knowledge base including:

  • Florida Bar Rules of Professional Conduct
  • Florida Bar formal ethics opinions on AI in legal practice
  • Selected Florida and federal court decisions on AI documents, privilege & discovery
  • Sample AI use certifications required by Florida courts
  • Secondary legal analysis on AI ethics in legal practice
⚠ This tool does not search the internet. It does not access live Florida Bar databases, real-time court dockets, or current legislative updates. It searches only its loaded knowledge base.

Knowledge base current as of: April 25, 2026. Bar opinions, court rules, and standing orders change frequently. Always verify any cited authority independently before relying on it.

Do Not Enter Client Information
This tool is for general Florida ethics research only. You must not enter:
  • Client names or any identifying information
  • Case numbers or court file numbers
  • Opposing party names
  • Facts specific to any pending or closed matter
  • Any information that could identify a client, even indirectly

If uploading a document, redact all client-identifying information before uploading — names, addresses, account numbers, dates of birth, and case numbers.

Entering client information into any third-party AI tool may implicate your obligations under Florida Bar Rule 4-1.6 and may affect privilege or work product protection. See Fla. Bar Ethics Op. 24-1 (Jan. 19, 2024); United States v. Heppner, S.D.N.Y. (2026).
